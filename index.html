About the Program

A public profile for our Summer 2026 exchange.

Taiwan Q-Fin Hub is a student cohort participating in Taiwan's Youth Overseas Dream Fund, supported by the Youth Development Administration, Ministry of Education.

In summer 2026, the cohort will visit the Greater New York area for an academic and professional exchange program focused on quantum computing, artificial intelligence, financial technology, and quantitative finance.

This website serves as a public profile of the cohort and the summer program. It helps professors, university staff, industry professionals, and community partners understand what the students are working on, while also giving participants a credible page to link to their learning experience on LinkedIn and other professional profiles.

Program Focus

Quantum Computing, AI & FinTech Exchange

Through academic visits, expert discussions, professional networking, and post-program reflection, students explore how emerging technologies connect with financial systems, research communities, and global innovation ecosystems.

Quantum Computing & AI Research Exposure

Exposure to hybrid quantum-classical computing, quantum algorithms, and AI research through academic visits, lab discussions, and technical briefings.

Financial Engineering Seminars

Seminars on financial engineering, quantitative finance, and AI in finance, including risk modeling, portfolio methods, and market applications.

Professional Networking

Professional exchange with industry practitioners, Taiwanese-American technology leaders, and community partners through TAITA East and related networks.

Learning Outcomes

Participants develop reports, presentations, and follow-up sharing that reflect what they learned from research exposure, professional discussions, and site visits.

Schedule Preview

5-Week Itinerary →

A five-week exchange program across academic, research, and professional communities in the Greater New York area.

Week 1
Research Orientation
Introduction to the research environment, academic visits, and initial discussions on financial engineering and hybrid quantum-classical computing.
Week 2
HQCC & Financial AI Workshops
Workshops and seminars on hybrid quantum-classical computing, financial AI, and related research topics.
Week 3
Research and Industry Exchange
Cross-campus academic exchange, technology lectures, and professional discussions with research and industry communities.
Week 4
Urban Finance and AI Residency
Interdisciplinary sessions connecting finance, AI, data systems, and urban university environments.
Week 5
Synthesis & Reflection
Reflection, learning outcome preparation, final discussions, and post-program networking.
